Fittbug

A health/lifestyle application that aims to make you a better version of yourself by using gamification and context aware location notifications. Fittbug monitors where you are and recommends healthy alternative places to eat, and if it detects that you're eating in that area you get rewarded in the form of coupons or special offers from sponsors. This application earned a place in the Top 10 applications at the GITEX Technology Week of 2014. I designed parts of the interface and also developed it for Android.

Lets Go

New to Dubai? Looking for places you haven't visisted yet? If yes,then check out Let's Go! It's an android application that makes use of the ARCGIS SDK & OSM to help the user get around Dubai by providing directions with different transport modes (Walking,Public Transit and by Car). It also features a scheduling section where the user can "pin" a location they want to visit and strike it out once they've visited. This application won first place at the GISWORX 2014 Developer Challenge.

Pulse

Made for the Decode Hackathon, this web application is aimed for people who want to know where the most interesting place in Dubai is right now. Using social media trends from Facebook and Twitter, we have a bubble that grows and grows according to the location of the tweets/posts, showing the most happening place in Dubai is - showing the 'Pulse' of Dubai. If you want to see a demo,message me! I'd love to show you.

Snapple

A java based desktop game made using LibGDX during the WISTEM hackathon. It's based on the idea of Scrabble and Snake,where the player must chase after letters to make up a word, the larger the word the more points you get. I designed and did the front-end development and debugging for the project.Our team one Most Educational Game for the hackathon.

Helga and the Zoinks

An endless runner game like Monster Dash made by using the Corona SDK. I designed the art for this game and also programmed it alongside my partner,Nigel Marshal. By scrolling through the sprites I've made it gave the appearance of a large and endless game. The ground was also randomly created, adding replayability and enjoyability to it. Because I used the Corona SDK, this could easily be ported to Android,IOS devices and Windows platforms.
